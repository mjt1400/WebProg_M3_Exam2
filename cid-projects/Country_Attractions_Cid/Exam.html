Wonders of Japan

Mt. Fuji

Mount Fuji, at 3,776.24 meters, is Japan's tallest peak, located on the island of Honsh. It is Asia's second-highest volcano and the world's seventh-highest island peak. Mount Fuji is a live stratovolcano that erupted last between 1707 and 1708. On clear days, the peak may be seen from around 100 kilometers southwest of Tokyo. Mount Fuji's remarkably symmetrical cone, which is covered in snow for nearly five months of the year, is a popular tourist destination and is frequently portrayed in art and photography.

Hiroshima

Hiroshima Prefecture is located in the southwest corner of Honshu, Japan's main island. It is located in the Chugoku-Shikoku Region, surrounded to the north by the Chugoku Mountains and to the south by the Seto Inland Sea. There are 14 cities and 9 towns in the prefecture. Hiroshima began as a fortress town on the ta River delta in 1598. Hiroshima grew swiftly after the Meiji Restoration in 1868, becoming a major metropolitan center and industrial powerhouse. Hiroshima was granted city status in 1889. During the imperial period, the city was a hub of military activity, with key involvement in the First Sino-Japanese War, the Russo-Japanese War, and both world wars. The city of Hiroshima was the first to be hit by a nuclear bomb.

Yoyogi Park

In Shibuya Tokyo Japan, Yoyogi Park is a park near to Harajuku Station and Meiji Shrine in Yoyogikamizonocho, Shibuya, Tokyo, Japan. The park is a renowned tourist attraction in Tokyo. It is notably active on Sundays, when Japanese rock music enthusiasts, jugglers, comedians, martial arts clubs, cosplayers, and other subculture and hobby organizations congregate there. Thousands Japanese people go to the park each spring for hanami, or cherry blossom viewing. Picnic spots, bike lanes, bike rentals, and public sport courts are all available in this beautifully designed park.

Tokyo

Tokyo is Japan's capital and the country's most populous prefecture. The prefecture is part of the Kant region on the central Pacific coast of Japan's main island, Honshu, and is located at the head of Tokyo Bay.

Yakushima

Yakushima, In Kagoshima Prefecture Japan, Yakushima is one of the Sumi Islands. The island has a population of 13,178 people and has an area of 504.88 km2. The island may be reached via hydrofoil boat, slow automobile ferry, or air from Yakushima Airport. Yakushima is the administrative center of the entire island. Kuchinoerabujima is also served by the town. The Kirishima-Yaku National Park encompasses the vast portion of the island.
